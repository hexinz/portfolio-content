What you can eat in Shanghai?

Family Food

Eating at home is always the most common, comfortable, and pleasant thing for Shanghainese. During the winter in 2021, when the COVID-19 is very severe at that time, my family cook and have meals together everyday for as long as two months. Every one of us think it is a precious reunion and a great opportunity for communication between family members. What's more, my mum's sister can cook so delicious food that I couldn't wait to see what food we have for tomorrow!

Sometimes, we also have barbeques in our garden. It is enjoyable to have meals with green plants around. But keep in mind that always be careful with the fire! Besides, having barbeques in Summer is also not a good idea due to the annoying mosquitos.

Shanghai people also love to have meals together, especially during festivals: Chinese New Year, Chinese National Holiday, etc. or birthday and other memorial ceremony for passed family members. Different holidays have different traditional food. For example, we usually have moon cakes in Chinese Dragon Boat Festival and dumplings in Chinese New Year. For the memorial ceremony, traditions vary quite differently in different locations in China. We usually have the so-called Ninbo Food for memorial ceremonies.
